# Law of large numbers

The [law of large numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_of_large_numbers) (LLN) states that random events tend to stabilise at the average value over time.

For example, rolling a 6-sided dice has an average of $\frac{(1+2+3+4+5+6)}{6}=3.5$. The first rolls will varie wildly; but more rolls will get the mean value closer to the average.
